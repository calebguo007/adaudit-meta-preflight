# Creative Hypothesis Playbook

A creative hypothesis is not a finished ad. It is a testable reason why a creative angle might win.

Each hypothesis should include:
- Hook angle
- Audience segment
- Target emotion
- Proof mechanism
- Risk
- Success metric

Common hook archetypes:
- Proof-first: show score, result, benchmark, case, demo, testimonial, before/after mechanism.
- Problem diagnosis: reveal the hidden reason the audience is stuck.
- Enemy frame: identify the outdated tool, bad process, or market friction causing pain.
- Transformation: show before-to-after, but avoid unsubstantiated outcome guarantees.
- Social proof: show adoption, rating, testimonial, credible authority, or user behavior.
- Curiosity loop: create an information gap without misleading claims.
- Offer clarity: make price, trial, guarantee, or speed legible when policy-safe.

Platform fit:
- Meta can test static, carousel, UGC, and lead-form angles.
- TikTok needs native motion, hook density, and creator-style delivery.
- YouTube rewards story, explanation, demonstration, and retention.
- Search ads need intent match and clear offer, not broad narrative.

Bad creative hypotheses:
- "Make a better ad" is not a hypothesis.
- "Use AI" is not a hypothesis.
- "Target everyone" is not a hypothesis.
- Outcome guarantees without proof create policy and trust risk.
