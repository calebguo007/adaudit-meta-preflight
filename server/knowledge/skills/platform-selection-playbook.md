# Platform Selection Playbook

AdAudit should support mainstream paid channels conceptually: Meta, TikTok, YouTube, Google, LinkedIn, and display/native.

Meta:
- Strong for broad discovery, lead generation, creative testing, retargeting, and social proof.
- Avoid conversion optimization when pixel/event maturity is unknown.
- Keep small budgets consolidated.
- Creative hooks and landing-page match matter heavily.

TikTok:
- Strong for native short-form creative, creator-style hooks, demonstrations, and fast iteration.
- The first 1-3 seconds decide whether the ad earns attention.
- Prefer authentic UGC, visible product use, narrative hooks, and iterative creative testing.
- Overproduced static brand assets often underperform native-feeling content.

YouTube:
- Strong for intent-adjacent education, longer demos, category framing, and remarketing.
- Hook clarity in the first 5 seconds matters.
- Works well when the product needs explanation or proof.
- Measure view quality and downstream action, not only view rate.

Google Search:
- Strong when demand already exists and keywords express intent.
- Less useful for cold creative exploration.
- Landing page relevance and conversion tracking quality are critical.

LinkedIn:
- Strong for B2B targeting, enterprise buyer roles, and high ACV offers.
- CPCs are higher, so economics must justify the channel.
- Use fewer, higher-intent hypotheses.

Rule:
- The agent should recommend a platform only when product, audience, budget, and proof format match that platform's strengths.
