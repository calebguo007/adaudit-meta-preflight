# Budget Signal and Unit Economics

Budget is not just a number; it determines which experiments are statistically useful.

General heuristics:
- Small first-flight budgets should avoid fragmented testing.
- Under roughly $1000, prefer one or two interpretable ad sets.
- Splitting a small budget across many audiences, placements, or creatives often produces noise, not learning.
- Budget density matters more than testing every idea at once.
- If a platform optimization event is scarce, do not optimize for it first.

Signal math:
- Estimate clicks from CPC ranges before planning variants.
- Estimate leads or conversions from realistic conversion rates, not from hope.
- If target CPA is provided, use 2.5x to 3x target CPA as an early kill threshold.
- If AOV, gross margin, LTV, or close rate are provided, calculate break-even CPA.
- CTR alone is not a scale signal. Check downstream value and lead quality.

Kill, hold, scale:
- Kill an ad set after enough spend without qualified signal.
- Hold changes during the first learning window unless delivery is broken.
- Scale gradually, often around 20%, only after efficiency and downstream quality are both acceptable.
